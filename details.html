500 families'special Christmas gifts

Melbourne,Australia

Brimbank, Broadmeadows, Dandenong, Epping and Werribee, 6400 toys.

More than 500 families in Melbourne have received special Christmas gifts from a volunteer team from The Smith Family. "For many people, Christmas is a time to celebrate, but we fear that some families will lose the joy of" The Smith Family director Anton Leschen said, "get these books, toys may be able to give these families to bring some hope that they can like christmas." In the city, Brimbank, Broadmeadows, Dandenong, Epping and Werribee, 6400 toys were distributed. Beryl, a member of the volunteer group, has worked for this Christmas charity for 16 years, and she says she loves the job. "This is the best time of the year, because I can spread happiness. We try to make people who are rich or poor can enjoy Christmas, so that every child can have a happy holiday. This is a great feeling. Other places like Melbourne, Victoria also has such charitable activities, children will receive gifts from santa. Hana and Vili were a pair of parents from a poor family, and their families were also helped by the charity. "I am very happy, thank you!" Vili and Hana said, "we're grateful that Santa Claus is really here. We can't afford gifts to children at this time, but I really appreciate The Smith Family to help us. " The Smith Family is a child charity to help poor children in Australia get a good education and create a good future for them.
